Quick Facts

Location

Turkey is a country that spans two continents. Most of its land is on the Anatolia peninsula in Asia, while a smaller part, Eastern Thrace, is in Europe. It is bordered by the Black Sea to the north, the Mediterranean Sea to the south, and the Aegean Sea to the west.

Population

Turkey's population was about 86.27 million in 2023. In 2024, it welcomed over 52.63 million foreign visitors, setting a new record for international arrivals and exceeding pre-pandemic levels.

Language

The official language, Turkish, is the first language spoken by 90% of the 71.1 million people. Minority languages include Kurdish, which is spoken by 6% of the population, but it is not recognized as an official language.

Currency

The Turkish lira is Turkey's official currency. Its abbreviation is TRY, and it is represented by the ₺ sign.

Sports

The most famous and popular sport in Turkey is football (soccer). However, the country also has a strong following for basketball, volleyball, and the traditional sport of oil wrestling.

Turkey is a dream destination waiting to be explored. It stands the perfect place to experience a mix of history, beauty, and adventure. From the lively streets of Istanbul and the breathtaking views of Cappadocia to the stunning white terraces of Pamukkale and the golden beaches of Antalya, every corner of Turkey has something magical to offer. Istanbul, once the capital of three great empires, the Roman, Byzantine, and Ottoman, captures the heart with its blend of European and Asian cultures, vibrant markets, and impressive architecture. Whether you’re exploring ancient ruins in Ephesus, relaxing by the coast in Bodrum, or discovering the charm of Troy and Ankara, Turkey has endless possibilities for every traveler. It’s the ultimate family vacation spot, filled with culture, excitement, and memories waiting to be made.

What makes Turkey truly special is its incredible diversity. Every region tells a different story. You can see fairy-tale hot air balloons floating above Cappadocia’s valleys or enjoy the peaceful turquoise waters along the Mediterranean coast. Turkish cuisine adds another layer of charm. It offers delicious dishes like kebabs, baklava, and traditional Turkish tea that show the country’s warm hospitality. Visitors can stroll through colorful bazaars, relax in ancient thermal baths, or admire centuries-old mosques and palaces. Beyond the sights and flavors, it’s the people of Turkey—their kindness, generosity, and pride in their culture—that make every visit unforgettable. Whether you seek adventure, relaxation, or cultural discovery, Turkey welcomes every traveler with open arms and promises lasting memories.

In every sense, Turkey is more than just a destination. It’s an experience that lingers long after the journey ends. Its breathtaking landscapes, ancient wonders, and vibrant culture create a place where history and modern life coexist in harmony. Whether you are wandering through Istanbul’s lively markets, watching the sunrise over Cappadocia, or enjoying a quiet evening by the sea, every moment in Turkey feels like a dream come to life. For anyone seeking adventure, connection, and beauty, Turkey is the dream destination waiting to be discovered.
